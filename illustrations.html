Illustrations

My love for artwork

I loved drawing ever since I was a little kid. My sister used to go to an art school, and she was the one who inspired me to pick up drawing. I was never good wtih drawing from imagination. I always preferred having the picture I am trying to draw in front of me. Since I grew up watching a lot of cartoons, I tend to draw these characters more than any other object. As I grew older, I got into the habit of watching Japanese animes. People who don't know anything about animes have a misconception that animes are like cartoons and are intended for a younger audience. This could not be further from the truth, as most animes have complex storylines which are hard for children to grasp. Some even contain adult content and gore, which are definitely not appropriate for any child! I have so much respect and appreciation for the artists and writers who create mangas and animes.

My first drawing experiences involved pencil sketches of influential figures. Later on, I started playing around on my desktop with Microsoft Paint. It took me a lot of time to complete my drawings in Paint, but I really enjoyed my work so that wasn't an issue for me. After recently discovering Adobe Illustrator, I decided to draw my favorite characters more accurately. I learned to draw 3-D pictures using shadows and shades of the same colors to account for different angles. Even though my Illustrator projects look decent enough, my technique is actually that of an amateur! Unlike conventional manga/anime artists who hand-draw the pictures first and then scan them, I do the entire process on my computer, using just the keyboard and the mouse. Some day I will probably change my ways and learn the way of the masters. For now, I will settle with what I have.
